## 📋 What is this tool?
Notebook-video turns your written scripts into high-quality 2K videos. This tool uses automation to handle the complex parts of video production. You provide a Chinese script, and the application generates a video complete with motion graphics, background speech, and accurate captions. It targets creators who want to produce consistent, repeatable explainer content without manual editing.

## 🚀 Creating your first video
These steps help you create your video from a script:

1. Open the notebook-video program from your desktop.
2. Select the "New Project" button.
3. Paste your Chinese script into the text area. 
4. Choose a voice profile from the settings menu. The software offers several options for clear, professional narration.
5. Review the motion graphic style. The tool suggests layouts that fit typical explainer video formats.
6. Click the "Generate" button. 
7. The application processes your script and builds the video in the background. You can track progress on the status bar.
8. When the process finishes, the software saves the final 2K video to your designated output folder.

## ❓ Frequently asked questions

Do I need skills in video editing software?
No. This tool automates the edit. You provide the text; the tool handles the visuals and timing.

Can I adjust the speed of the narrator?
Yes. Open the settings menu to move the speed slider to your preferred pace.

What format does the video use?
The tool exports standard MP4 files compatible with all major video platforms.

Where do the video files go?
The program creates a folder named "Output" within your project directory. You can change this path in the program settings.

Is my data private?
Your scripts stay on your local machine. The tool interacts with cloud services only to generate the synthetic speech and optimize caption placement.

What happens if the render fails?
Check your internet connection first. If the problem continues, restart the software and retry the generation process from the main menu.

## 🔧 Troubleshooting tips
* Ensure you have read and write permissions for the folder where you installed the application.
* Keep your computer plugged into power if you are working on long scripts to prevent interruptions.
* Close other memory-intensive programs while the video renders to speed up the process.
* Check the "Logs" folder in the application directory if you experience repeated errors.
